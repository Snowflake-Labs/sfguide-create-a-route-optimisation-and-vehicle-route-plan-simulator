# Retail Catchment Analysis

Welcome to the **Retail Catchment Analysis** - a powerful geospatial analytics solution that helps businesses identify optimal retail locations by analyzing customer catchment areas, market penetration, and competitive landscapes.

## Overview

The Retail Catchment Analysis leverages advanced geospatial data science to provide data-driven insights for retail expansion and location optimization. This solution combines demographic data, foot traffic patterns, and competitive analysis to identify the most promising retail locations.

## Getting Started

### Accessing the Analysis Tool

1. **Navigate to Applications** - From your Snowflake interface, go to the Apps section
2. **Launch Retail Catchment** - Click on the Retail Catchment Analysis application
3. **Select Analysis Type** - Choose from catchment mapping, location scoring, or market analysis
4. **Configure Parameters** - Set your target demographics, radius, and analysis criteria

### Key Analysis Types

#### Catchment Mapping
- Define catchment areas using drive time, walking distance, or demographic criteria
- Overlay competitor locations to identify market gaps
- Analyze customer density and spending patterns within each zone

#### Location Scoring
- Input potential retail locations for automated scoring
- Compare multiple locations side-by-side
- Get ranked recommendations based on your business criteria

#### Market Analysis
- Generate comprehensive market reports for specific regions
- Analyze demographic trends and consumer behavior patterns
- Identify emerging market opportunities

## Best Practices

### Data Preparation
- Ensure your customer data includes geographic coordinates
- Validate address data quality for accurate geocoding
- Include relevant demographic and transaction data for enhanced analysis

### Analysis Configuration
- Set appropriate catchment radii based on your retail category
- Consider local transportation patterns and geographic barriers
- Include seasonal variations in foot traffic and demographics

### Interpretation Guidelines
- Combine quantitative scores with local market knowledge
- Consider long-term demographic trends, not just current data
- Factor in upcoming developments and infrastructure changes
